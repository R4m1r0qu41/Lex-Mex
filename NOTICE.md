# Notices and legal-source attribution

## Project status

Lex-Mex is an independent, experimental open-source project. It is not an
official publication, is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Cámara de
Diputados, Diario Oficial de la Federación, CNBV, or any other Mexican public
authority, and does not provide legal advice.

Users must verify all legal text, effective dates, external events, and legal
conclusions against the official sources cited in the corpus.

## Official legal texts

The repository includes excerpts and structured reproductions of Mexican laws,
decrees, and other official materials for provenance, analysis, and technical
testing. Article 14(VIII) of Mexico's *Ley Federal del Derecho de Autor*
addresses legislative, regulatory, administrative, and judicial texts and
their official translations. Lex-Mex does not claim copyright in official
legal text and does not relicense it under the software licenses.

Reproductions should remain faithful to the official text and retain their
source attribution. The source manifests and canonical records identify the
publisher, URL, retrieval data, and cryptographic hashes used by the pipeline.

Original software, schemas, documentation, annotations, metadata arrangement,
and other copyrightable project contributions are licensed under
`MIT OR Apache-2.0`, at the recipient's option. Generated Markdown may contain
both official source text and original Lex-Mex structure or annotations; each
part retains its applicable legal status.
